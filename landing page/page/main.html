P L A N E T S

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and the smallest in the solar system. It has a rocky surface with extreme temperature variations, ranging from scorching hot during the day to freezing cold at night. Its thin atmosphere, composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, and hydrogen, offers little protection from solar radiation.

Venus

Venus is similar in size and structure to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere primarily made of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid. This dense atmosphere causes a runaway greenhouse effect, making Venus the hottest planet in the solar system, with surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life. It has a balanced atmosphere that contains oxygen and nitrogen, liquid water on its surface, and a magnetic field that protects it from harmful solar radiation.Earth's climate and ecosystems have been profoundly shaped by its.

Mars

Known as the "Red Planet" due to its iron oxide-rich surface, Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun. It has a thin atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide, with frigid temperatures and large dust storms. Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and evidence suggests it once had liquid water.

Jupitar

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. It has a thick atmosphere with dynamic storms, including the Great Red Spot, a massive storm larger than Earth. Jupiter's strong magnetic field and over 80 moons.

Saturn

Saturn is known for its spectacular ring system, composed of ice and rock particles. It is the second-largest planet in the solar system and a gas giant like Jupiter, mostly made up of hydrogen and helium. Saturn has numerous moons, with Titan being the largest and the only moon.

Uranus

Uranus is an ice giant with a pale blue color due to methane in its atmosphere. It has a unique rotational tilt that causes it to rotate on its side, leading to extreme seasonal variations. Uranus has faint rings and 27 known moons, with its atmosphere composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and methane.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and another ice giant. It is known for its intense blue color, caused by the presence of methane, and its strong winds, which are the fastest in the solar system. Neptune has a dynamic atmosphere with large storms, faint rings, and 14 known moons, the largest being Triton.

A S T E R O I D S

CERES

Biggest Asteroid

Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter and is classified as a dwarf planet. It was discovered by Giuseppe Piazzi in 1801 and is named after the Roman goddess of agriculture and fertility. Ceres has a diameter of about 940 kilometers (about 584 miles), making it the smallest recognized dwarf planet in our solar system.

VESTA

Second-largest Asteroid

Vesta is the second-largest object in the asteroid belt and one of the most massive. It has a diameter of about 525 kilometers (326 miles). Discovered in 1807 by Heinrich Wilhelm Olbers, Vesta has a differentiated structure with a core, mantle, and crust, similar to that of terrestrial planets. It is notable for its large impact basins.Vesta has a diameter of about 525 kilometers.

PALLAS

Third-largest Asteroid

Pallas is the third-largest asteroid in the asteroid belt, with a diameter of about 512 kilometers (318 miles). Discovered by Heinrich Wilhelm Olbers in 1802, Pallas is unusual due to its highly inclined orbit compared to most other asteroids. It is composed mainly of silicate materials and may have a partially differentiated interior. Pallas's orbit and composition.
